I need to materially improve the trustworthiness and reasoning discipline of the Growth Plan / Strategy Page generation engine.

Important:
This is not a redesign.
The structure and UI can largely remain as they are.
The problem is in how the reasoning layer constructs and presents information.

Core issue:
The current strategy output is blending observed data, inferred reasoning, modeled assumptions, and fallback/default logic into a single narrative voice and presenting them with too much equal confidence.

This makes parts of the strategy feel credible, while other parts feel overstated, inaccurate, or falsely precise.

Goal:
Tighten the content-generation logic so the Growth Plan becomes:
- more grounded
- more trustworthy
- more deal-aware
- less reliant on overconfident modeled numbers
- still premium, persuasive, and conversion-oriented

What must change:

1. Introduce internal claim classification
Every meaningful data point / claim / metric in the strategy engine should be classified as one of:
- observed
- inferred
- modeled
- insufficient

This classification should directly control wording and output behaviour.

2. Apply rendering rules by classification
Observed:
- use direct factual language
- only for things directly supported by evidence

Inferred:
- use interpretation language such as:
- suggests
- indicates
- likely
- appears to

Modeled:
- present only as scenarios or directional estimates
- use ranges where possible
- never present modeled numbers as fixed facts

Insufficient:
- do not force precision
- avoid confident outputs when evidence is too weak

3. Remove false precision
The strategy should avoid exact-looking numbers unless they are clearly grounded.
Do not present precise search demand, enquiry, revenue, or lost-opportunity numbers without:
- a clear source
- a visible calculation path internally
- sufficient evidence quality
- a confidence threshold

Prefer approximate ranges and scenario language instead.

4. Rewrite modeled sections to acknowledge assumptions
Instead of outputs like:
- 120 additional enquiries per month
Use:
- If visibility improved and the business captured a modest share of available demand, this could represent approximately 8–12 additional enquiries per month depending on conversion performance and positioning.

The point is not to weaken the page.
It is to make it honest and therefore more persuasive.

5. Remove unsafe absolute language
Replace statements like:
- 0 visibility
- 0 rankings
- 0 presence
unless they are explicitly proven

Use safer alternatives such as:
- limited observed visibility
- no strong evidence of ranking presence
- weak observed local search presence

6. Prioritise the highest-risk sections first
Audit and tighten these sections first:
- Market Opportunity
- Cost of Inaction
- Projected Outcomes
- KPI Table

These are the biggest trust-risk areas if the numbers are weakly grounded.

7. Preserve and refine the stronger narrative sections
These sections are directionally stronger and should be improved, not replaced:
- visibility gaps
- website structure issues
- strategic direction
- roadmap/phases
- growth pillars

8. Enforce a structured reasoning pattern in each section
Each strategy section should follow:
- evidence
- interpretation
- strategic implication
- recommended move

This should make the strategy feel grounded and commercially useful rather than generic or formulaic.

9. Improve deal awareness
The strategy should use more than website/search evidence.
It should better reflect:
- conversation context
- client priorities
- deal context
- current business situation
- what the client appears to care about

The output should feel specific to the deal, not just the website.

10. Align scenario modeling with the Live Strategy Simulator
Static strategy sections should introduce opportunity directionally and conservatively.
Interactive scenario modeling should live primarily in the Live Strategy Simulator.

Do not let static sections overstate modeled outcomes as fixed truths.

What I want from you:
1. Audit the current Growth Plan generation logic
2. Identify which sections are currently:
- observed-driven
- inferred-driven
- modeled/default-heavy
3. Refactor the highest-risk sections so modeled outputs are clearly directional/scenario-based
4. Introduce internal provenance/confidence handling for claims and numbers
5. Preserve the existing premium structure/UI while improving trust and reasoning quality

Success criteria:
- the Growth Plan clearly distinguishes fact from assumption
- modeled outputs feel honest and commercially credible
- false precision is reduced
- absolute unsupported statements are removed
- the plan remains persuasive and premium without overstating certainty
- the strategy feels more aligned to the actual deal, not just generic website analysis
