There is a trust-breaking bug in the new ClientIntelligencePanel.

Observed behaviour:
For a client like “Our Sparky”, the panel is saying:
- no online presence
- no website signals detected
- no GBP/local signals detected
- no search signals detected
- no social profiles detected

But in reality, this client does have:
- a website
- social media
- GBP
- and existing online presence

Problem:
The client intelligence layer is presenting absence as fact when the underlying presence data is either not being loaded, not being mapped correctly, or not being reused from existing lead/client intelligence.

Goal:
Fix the client intelligence presence layer so it accurately reflects known existing digital presence and does not default to false “no signals detected” states.

What I want investigated:
1. Identify what data sources the ClientIntelligencePanel / intelligence-brief endpoint currently uses for:
- website presence
- GBP/local presence
- search signals
- social profiles
2. Compare that against the lead-side evidence/presence model, which appears to have richer and more accurate presence detection
3. Determine whether legacy clients are missing:
- carried-over lead evidence
- sourceIntelligence mapping
- client-side audit hydration
- website/social/GBP field mapping
4. Fix the client intelligence layer so it reuses known existing presence before falling back to “no signals detected”

Required product behaviour:
- If a website URL is known, do not say “No signals detected”
- If GBP is known or linked, do not say “No signals detected”
- If social profiles are known from lead data or prior audits, surface them
- If evidence is incomplete, use honest language like:
- “Not yet verified in client audit”
- “Existing channels not yet fully mapped”
- “Presence data incomplete — refresh audit”
- Do not present absence as fact unless absence is strongly evidenced

Implementation guidance:
1. Reuse existing lead-side/sourceIntelligence/client fields wherever possible
2. Merge known carried-over signals with fresh client audit signals
3. Only show true “no signals detected” states when the system has strong reason to believe that is correct
4. Distinguish between:
- known present
- observed in fresh audit
- not yet verified
- genuinely not found

Success criteria:
- clients with existing website / GBP / social presence show that presence correctly in Client Intelligence
- legacy clients no longer look like they have zero digital footprint when they do not
- client intelligence feels trustworthy and reality-aligned
