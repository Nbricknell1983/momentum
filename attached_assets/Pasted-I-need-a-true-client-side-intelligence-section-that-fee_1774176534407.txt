I need a true client-side intelligence section that feels much closer to the lead-side Deal Intelligence / Agent Intelligence experience.

Problem:
Right now the client workspace (example: Our Sparky) shows a Growth Audit plus mostly empty engine panels. It does not provide the rich, structured, strategic intelligence layer that exists on the lead side (example: First Parking).

This creates a continuity problem:
- leads have deep intelligence
- clients lose that intelligence feel and drop into a lighter audit/status experience

That is the wrong direction.

Goal:
Add a `Client Intelligence` section at the top of the client workspace that acts as the client-side equivalent of Deal Intelligence, but adapted for execution and preservation.

Product rule:
The client workspace should not just show what we plan to activate.
It should show:
- what online presence already exists
- what is working now
- what is weak
- what must be preserved
- what the execution strategy is
- what agents are actively doing next

Recommended section name:
- Client Intelligence
(alternative: Execution Intelligence / Growth Execution Intelligence)

What I want in Client Intelligence:
1. Presence Snapshot
- website
- GBP / Maps
- search
- social
- paid search
- current observed signals
- what already exists online

2. Customer / Market Context
- target customer
- search intent themes
- commercial context

3. Website Interpretation
- what the current site is doing well
- what is weak
- what affects conversion
- what existing SEO value should be preserved

4. Opportunities
- biggest immediate growth opportunities
- strongest execution upside

5. Key Gaps / Risks
- what is missing
- what is weak
- what could be lost during rebuild/migration if not handled carefully

6. Commercial / Execution Angle
- how Website + SEO + GBP fit together for this client
- what the actual execution strategy is now

7. Delivery Priorities
- what should happen first
- what agents are doing now
- what comes next

Important preservation requirement:
If a client already has a live website, the section must explicitly surface SEO-preservation intelligence, such as:
- pages/URLs worth preserving
- service/location pages with likely equity
- redirects likely required in a rebuild
- metadata/schema worth keeping
- current GBP/search visibility that should not be lost

Implementation guidance:
1. Reuse the existing lead-side intelligence/presence model where possible
2. Transform it into a client-facing execution/preservation version instead of rebuilding from scratch
3. Place this section at the top of the center column above the engine panels
4. Make it rich and structured like the lead-side intelligence experience, not a lightweight audit summary
5. Ensure it works for legacy/non-activated clients too, not just newly activated ones

Success criteria:
- client workspace gets a rich intelligence section comparable to the lead experience
- users can see what digital presence already exists
- SEO-preservation risks are visible before website rebuild work begins
- client strategy and execution feel connected
- the client page feels intelligent and proactive, not like a downgraded audit shell
