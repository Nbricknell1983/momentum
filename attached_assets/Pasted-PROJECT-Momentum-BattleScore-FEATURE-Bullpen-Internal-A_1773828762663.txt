PROJECT: Momentum / BattleScore
FEATURE: Bullpen (Internal AI Workforce Command Layer)
PRIORITY: High
TYPE: Internal operator feature only — NOT client-facing

OBJECTIVE:
Build an internal-only “Bullpen” layer inside Momentum that gives staff/operator visibility and control over the AI workforce running work across leads, deals, and clients.

Bullpen is NOT a gimmick, avatar system, or client-facing novelty.
Bullpen is the internal command layer that shows:
- what AI roles/functions are working on
- what is active right now
- what is queued
- what is blocked
- what needs approval
- what has completed
- what automation rules are in force

This feature must solve the current visibility/control gap:
Right now, work may be happening in the background, but from inside Momentum there is not enough clarity around:
- what the system is doing
- which functional engine is responsible
- whether work is active / idle / blocked
- what requires intervention
- whether automation is respecting work-hour rules and approval guardrails

Bullpen should make Momentum feel like an AI-operated business system with managerial oversight.

NON-NEGOTIABLE GUARDRAILS:
1. Bullpen is internal only
2. Do NOT expose Bullpen or agent-role UI in client-facing surfaces
3. Do NOT turn this into a gimmicky “AI characters” system
4. Use functional role names, not personalities
5. Preserve multi-tenant isolation
6. Extend current Momentum architecture without breaking Pipeline, Nurture, Clients, Client Detail, AI Sales Engine, AI Client Growth Engine, or public strategy URL flows
7. Do NOT create duplicate task systems if existing activity/log/task objects can be extended safely

CORE PRODUCT PRINCIPLE:
Clients should see outcomes and progress.
Internal operators should see the workforce and the work.

BULLPEN DEFINITION:
Bullpen = internal AI workforce management and execution visibility layer.

It should represent functional specialist engines such as:
- Sales Specialist
- SEO Specialist
- Website Specialist
- Google Ads Specialist
- GBP Specialist
- Social Specialist
- Client Growth Specialist
- Business Strategist
- Review / Reputation Specialist
- Automation / Operations Specialist

These are NOT separate client-facing personas.
These are internal responsibility domains mapped to specialist engines/jobs.

PRIMARY USER:
- Nathan / internal operators / staff / admins / sales leaders / account managers

SECONDARY USER:
- future internal team members who need to understand system activity quickly

DO NOT:
- add cartoon AI avatars
- add fake chatty bios
- build novelty “meet your AI team” UI
- fragment the product into disconnected agent pages
- expose internal workforce language to clients

USE CASES TO SUPPORT:
1. Internal operator opens Momentum and instantly sees:
   - what AI work is happening across the system
   - what needs attention
   - what is waiting for approval
   - what is blocked
   - what was completed recently

2. Internal operator opens a client and can see:
   - what roles/engines are currently working on that account
   - what jobs are in flight
   - what is queued next
   - what intervention is required

3. Internal operator can trust that:
   - AI is respecting business hours
   - AI is not sending email/SMS/calls outside allowed windows
   - high-risk actions are not auto-executing without approval

4. Internal operator can identify bottlenecks:
   - SEO blocked due to missing service data
   - Ads waiting approval
   - GBP stalled due to no review activity
   - Website rollout waiting on brand assets

FEATURE STRUCTURE:
Build Bullpen as an internal command layer with 4 main parts:

PART 1 — BULLPEN OVERVIEW
PART 2 — WORKFORCE BY FUNCTION
PART 3 — ATTENTION / BLOCKERS / APPROVALS
PART 4 — AUTOMATION RULES / CONTROL

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE:
Bullpen should be accessible from the internal app navigation.
Do NOT place it in client-facing public pages.
Possible placement:
- Management section
- new “Bullpen” item in left nav under Management
- or nested in Management / Operations if needed

Keep discoverability high for internal users.

PART 1 — BULLPEN OVERVIEW
Create a top-level overview panel that answers:
- What is happening right now?
- How much is active?
- Where are the issues?
- What needs a decision?

Required summary metrics:
- Active Workloads
- Queued Jobs
- Awaiting Approval
- Blocked Jobs
- Completed Today
- Failed / Needs Retry
- Clients with Active AI Work
- Leads / Deals / Clients affected today

Optional but useful:
- By business domain (sales, seo, web, ads, gbp, growth)
- By priority
- By tenant count (internal only, aggregated safely)

PART 2 — WORKFORCE BY FUNCTION
Show Bullpen by functional role, not by gimmick personality.

Each role card/panel should include:
- Role name
- Status: Active / Idle / Awaiting Approval / Blocked / Needs Attention
- Current work items
- Queue
- Last completed action
- Current client/lead/deal count affected
- Issues/blockers summary

Initial functional roles to support:
1. Sales Specialist
2. SEO Specialist
3. Website Specialist
4. Google Ads Specialist
5. GBP Specialist
6. Client Growth Specialist
7. Review / Reputation Specialist
8. Strategy Specialist
9. Operations / Automation Specialist

Example internal role behavior:
Sales Specialist
- drafting follow-ups
- preparing call prep
- scheduling next actions
- moving deals when logic supports it

SEO Specialist
- generating service/location page plans
- preparing internal linking improvements
- monitoring ranking target gaps
- recommending/queuing SEO rollout jobs

Website Specialist
- preparing sitemap/build structures
- publishing or preparing page updates
- resolving website build dependencies

Google Ads Specialist
- preparing campaigns
- reviewing keyword targeting
- awaiting launch approval
- monitoring campaign issues/opportunities

GBP Specialist
- surfacing maps authority gaps
- preparing service/category/review recommendations
- flagging local visibility issues

Client Growth Specialist
- monitoring client health
- surfacing expansion opportunities
- flagging churn/watchlist accounts

PART 3 — ATTENTION / BLOCKERS / APPROVALS
This is one of the most important sections.
Create a consolidated area called something like:
- Needs Attention
- Blockers & Approvals
- Action Required

This must surface high-value operational issues across all roles.

Examples:
- SEO blocked: missing service inputs for 3 clients
- Ads awaiting approval: 2 campaigns
- GBP stagnating: no review growth in 14 days
- Website rollout paused: assets not uploaded
- Follow-up sequence prepared but outside business hours
- Job failed: strategy generation retry required

Each issue must show:
- issue title
- affected client/lead/deal
- responsible functional role
- severity
- recommended next action
- CTA (approve, retry, open record, dismiss, snooze)

PART 4 — AUTOMATION RULES / CONTROL
This is mandatory because the system must not run wild outside workday or approval guardrails.

Create a visible internal control section for Bullpen that shows:
- current automation mode
- allowed operating hours
- communication restrictions
- approval requirements
- last rule change
- who changed it

Support at minimum:
- work hours window (e.g. 8:00am–5:30pm local time)
- no SMS outside work hours
- no email outside work hours unless explicitly allowed
- no outbound calls outside work hours
- require approval for campaign launches
- require approval for publish actions
- require approval for high-risk communications
- allow different settings by domain (sales/marketing/operations) later if needed

This should visually reassure the operator that the AI workforce is constrained correctly.

BULLPEN UX PRINCIPLES:
- clear
- operational
- managerial
- not playful
- not noisy
- prioritised by actionability
- use status chips, compact summaries, and drill-down detail
- default view should answer “what needs my attention?”
- internal operators should be able to scan it in under 30 seconds

UI DESIGN REQUIREMENTS:
1. Top summary row/cards
2. “Needs Attention” section near top
3. Functional role cards or grouped panels below
4. Each role panel expandable to show:
   - current jobs
   - queued jobs
   - recent actions
   - blockers
5. Add filters:
   - role
   - status
   - priority
   - client / region / tenant if appropriate
6. Add time filter:
   - now
   - today
   - last 7 days
7. Include click-through links into the affected lead/deal/client record
8. Use the existing Momentum design language and component patterns
9. Do not create a visually disconnected admin app

CLIENT DETAIL INTEGRATION:
In addition to the main Bullpen page, add a compact Bullpen section to internal client detail view.

This compact section should show:
- which functional roles are currently assigned/active on this client
- active jobs
- queued jobs
- blocked items
- approvals needed
- recent AI actions on this client

This should complement, not replace:
- Client Health Score
- Service Performance
- Retention Signals
- Expansion Opportunities
- Local GBP Rankings
- AI Client Growth Engine

Think of it as the internal workforce view for that account.

RELATIONSHIP TO EXECUTION ENGINE:
Bullpen is the visibility/control layer over the execution engine.

Execution engine = work being done
Bullpen = internal oversight of who/what is doing the work

Do NOT merge them into one confusing concept.
Do keep them tightly connected.

Use a shared job model underneath:
- role/function responsible
- entity type (lead, deal, client)
- entity id
- job type
- status
- priority
- startedAt
- queuedAt
- completedAt
- blockedReason
- requiresApproval
- approvalStatus
- lastHeartbeat
- result summary

DATA MODEL / SAFE EXTENSIONS:
Extend existing collections safely.
Add or extend support for concepts such as:
- bullpenRoles
- executionJobs
- aiActions
- approvals
- automationRules
- automationWindows
- workerHeartbeats (if needed)
- blockers
- retries / failures

Each relevant record must include:
- orgId
- entityType
- entityId
- role/function
- status
- timestamps
- createdBy / triggeredBy where relevant

Do not duplicate if current agentTasks / agentLogs / executionEvents already partially cover this.
Prefer extending existing patterns.

STATUS MODEL:
Standardize status values where possible:
- Idle
- Active
- Queued
- Awaiting Approval
- Blocked
- Needs Attention
- Completed
- Failed

For role summary cards, derive the visible status from underlying job states.

AI ACTIONS FEED:
Bullpen must reuse or extend the existing AI action logging model.
Show human-readable action entries such as:
- Prepared 8 suburb pages for SEO rollout
- Drafted Google Ads campaign for approval
- Detected weak location intent coverage on client website
- Queued review acquisition workflow
- Paused outbound follow-up due to work-hour rules

Do not dump raw technical event logs.
Translate logs into readable operator summaries.

APPROVAL SYSTEM:
Bullpen must integrate with approvals clearly.

Support:
- approve
- reject
- edit / review first
- snooze
- retry

Show why approval is required:
- outside work hours
- medium/high-risk action
- campaign launch
- publish action
- communication action
- missing data uncertainty

WORK-HOUR ENFORCEMENT:
Critical requirement:
The UI and job system must support workday restrictions so AI does not send messages or act outside approved windows.

At minimum support:
- account-level or org-level local business hours
- queue work outside hours instead of executing immediately
- show queued reason in Bullpen
- surface “held until work hours” state where appropriate

Example:
“Follow-up email prepared — held until 8:00am due to communication rules”

This is extremely important for trust and control.

MANAGEMENT VALUE:
Bullpen should feel like:
- a manager walking the floor of an AI-operated office
- seeing who is working on what
- seeing where problems are
- making decisions when needed

It should NOT feel like:
- a dev console
- a raw job queue
- a technical agent monitor only engineers can understand

PHASED DELIVERY ORDER:
Build in the safest order:

PHASE 1
- Bullpen nav entry
- Bullpen overview
- summary counts
- needs attention section
- role cards with mock/live derived summaries from existing logs if available

PHASE 2
- connect to execution jobs / AI actions / approvals
- add client-level compact bullpen panel
- add status filters and drill-down

PHASE 3
- automation rules section
- work-hour enforcement visibility
- approval queue actions
- blocker resolution workflows

PHASE 4
- richer workload metrics
- worker heartbeat / job retry / failure visibility
- role efficiency insights

DO NOT try to build an overly elaborate simulation layer first.
Start with useful operational visibility and control.

QA / REGRESSION REQUIREMENTS:
Test for:
1. No Bullpen UI exposed in client-facing views
2. No tenant leakage in aggregated role/job summaries
3. Correct role/status mapping
4. Approvals and blockers visible and actionable
5. Work-hour restrictions represented correctly
6. Click-through from Bullpen to affected records works
7. Existing Pipeline / Nurture / Clients / Client Detail flows remain intact
8. No duplicate job rendering from repeated triggers
9. No misleading statuses when no actual work exists
10. Bullpen remains readable and performant with large job volumes

SUCCESS CRITERIA:
Bullpen is successful when an internal operator can open Momentum and within 30 seconds understand:
- what AI work is happening
- which functional roles are active
- what is blocked
- what needs approval
- what is waiting for business hours
- what has completed recently
- where to intervene next

FINAL PRODUCT INTENT:
Bullpen should make Momentum feel like an AI-operated growth firm with internal workforce visibility and control, while keeping the client experience simple, premium, and outcome-focused.
