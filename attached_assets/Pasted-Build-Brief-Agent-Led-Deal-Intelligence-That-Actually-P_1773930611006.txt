Build Brief — Agent-Led Deal Intelligence That Actually Prepares the Call and Drives the Win
The current lead/deal intelligence is still too empty and too passive.

When I open a lead like Panel Beaters Brisbane, I should not be doing the work of figuring out the business manually from a half-empty card. The agents should already have done that work.

Core requirement
The agents need to take ownership of lead and client intelligence so that when I open a record, I immediately see a commercially useful point of view — not an empty shell with a few fields and buttons.

I want the agents to act like an agency strategy team that has already researched the business and is briefing me before I ever touch the record.

What I want the agents to do
For every active lead — and later every client where relevant — the agents should automatically pull together:

1. Business understanding
what the business appears to do
what services it likely offers
what type of customer it likely serves
what geographic/service area it likely targets
what problem the business is trying to solve for its customers
2. Customer / search-intent understanding
For example, if the lead is related to search intent like:

“smash repairs Brisbane”
the agents should infer and explain:

why a customer searches that phrase
what job the customer is trying to get done
what urgency/emotion is likely involved
what trust/conversion signals matter for that customer
3. Website / presence interpretation
Not just whether a website exists.

The agents should explain:

what the current website appears to be trying to do
who it appears to be for
where it is weak
what it is missing
what opportunity exists commercially
4. Call preparation
I want the prep to tell me:

what to ask
what to test
what to challenge
what commercial angle to lead with
what likely opportunity exists
what likely gap is costing them leads or trust
5. Strategy and win logic
After the prep call, the agents should help:

generate a strong strategy
frame the opportunity commercially
support follow-up designed to win the work
6. Public strategy page
The strategy should be publishable to a public URL, but it must preserve the same structure and layout hierarchy the current strategy already uses. Do not redesign the strategy format for the public page.

7. Delivery conversion
If the strategy is accepted:

selected services/scope should automatically convert into real delivery work
owner and supporting specialists should be assigned
implementation should begin like a real agency team would handle it
The real problem to solve
Right now, the system is still too dependent on:

manual clicks
weak enrichment
empty sections
buttons like “build website” or “upload ahrefs” before the agents have even formed a strong point of view
That is backwards.

I want the agents to do the research and synthesis first, so the record opens with real intelligence already present.

Required behavior for lead intelligence
When I open a lead card, the intelligence section should already answer things like:

what this business probably does
who their likely customers are
what those customers are looking for
what the website is trying to do
whether the website is solving the right problem
what commercial opportunity is most likely here
what should happen next
For example, for Panel Beaters Brisbane, I want the system thinking like this:

this is likely a crash repair / smash repair / body repair business
the customer is likely distressed, time-sensitive, insurance-involved, and trust-sensitive
search intent is likely urgent and local
the website needs to reduce uncertainty and drive contact quickly
the opportunity is likely around trust, conversion clarity, local visibility, and follow-up
That kind of reasoning should already exist in the record before I start the call.

Required architecture change
Shift from:

field-filling and light enrichment
to:

agent-led commercial interpretation and prep
The system should not just populate:

website
socials
GBP
category
It should synthesize those into:

business understanding
customer understanding
opportunity understanding
prep call strategy
next best action
Required outputs per lead
For each active lead, I want a first-class Prep Call Pack that includes:

business snapshot
likely customer/jobs-to-be-done
search intent interpretation
website interpretation
trust/conversion observations
local visibility observations
likely opportunity areas
recommended discovery questions
recommended commercial angle
what is still unknown and should be asked on the call
Important
If some hard data is missing, the prep pack should still be strong.
The agents must use:

what is known
what is inferable
what should be asked next
Do not let missing data collapse the usefulness of the pack.

Required distinction
The system must separate:

factual fields
website, socials, location, reviews, etc.
from

commercial intelligence
what the business is trying to do
what the customer is trying to solve
where the opportunity is
what should happen on the call
The second category is the important one.

That is what is currently too weak.

Public strategy requirement
When a strategy is generated after the call:

it must be publishable to a public URL
that public page must preserve the same structure and layout hierarchy the current strategy already uses
the public page should be a shareable web-native version of the current strategy, not a redesigned format
Acceptance and delivery requirement
If the strategy is accepted:

website / SEO / GBP / ads / CRM / other accepted scope should convert into real delivery work automatically
the right specialist owners and supporting roles must be assigned
Bullpen should begin implementation sequencing
I do not want:

strategy as a dead-end artifact
I want:

strategy becoming delivery
Priority implementation order
Build this in the right order:

Phase 1
Strengthen the Prep Call Pack so every active lead opens with real commercial intelligence, not empty sections.

Phase 2
Generate stronger post-call strategy from that intelligence.

Phase 3
Publish to public strategy URL using the same current strategy structure/layout.

Phase 4
Capture acceptance.

Phase 5
Convert accepted scope into real implementation work.

Success criteria
This is successful when:

I can open a lead like Panel Beaters Brisbane and immediately get a strong prep-call point of view
the agents tell me what they think the business does, who it serves, what the website is trying to do, and where the opportunity is
the prep is useful even if some fields are missing
the strategy helps win the work
the public strategy page preserves the current strategy structure
accepted strategy items become real delivery work automatically
Final product rule
The agents should not make me do the thinking first.

They should walk into the lead before I do, research the business, form a commercially useful point of view, prepare the call, help win the work, and then turn accepted strategy into delivery.
