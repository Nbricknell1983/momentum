I want the client workspace to preserve the value of Deal Intelligence, but evolve it into an execution-oriented intelligence layer after conversion.

Problem:
Leads currently have strong Deal Intelligence that explains the strategy, opportunity, and next moves. But once a lead becomes a client, the workspace shifts too quickly into execution/status mode and loses the strategic intelligence layer that explains what we are doing and why.

Goal:
Add a client-side intelligence layer that acts like Deal Intelligence for execution.

Product direction:
Leads should have Deal Intelligence.
Clients should have Execution Intelligence (or Growth Execution Intelligence).

This should sit above the client workstreams and help the team understand:
- what strategy was sold
- what scope was selected
- what the priorities are
- what the agents should work on first
- how Website + SEO + GBP work together
- what the next delivery moves are

Example use case:
If a client has purchased:
- Website
- SEO
- GBP optimisation

The client workspace should clearly show that this is one coordinated visibility strategy, not three isolated workstreams.

What I want:
1. Add a new top-of-client-workspace intelligence panel
2. Base it on:
- preserved lead intelligence (`sourceIntelligence`)
- selected scope / activation plan
- current execution workstream state
3. Make it execution-oriented rather than sales-oriented
4. Keep it proactive so agents can use it to guide delivery and optimisation

Suggested section name:
- Growth Execution Intelligence
or
- Client Execution Intelligence

Suggested content structure:
1. Strategy summary
- a concise explanation of the growth strategy for this client

2. Scope sold
- Website
- SEO
- GBP
- Ads
(show only selected scope items)

3. Execution priorities
- what should happen first
- what should happen second
- what dependencies exist

4. Active agent focus
- what the agents are currently generating or improving
- what is waiting on review or prerequisites

5. Next delivery moves
- clear recommended next actions for the internal team

6. Visibility objective
- what business outcome this execution is trying to improve
- examples:
- local search visibility
- conversion rate
- map pack presence
- enquiries

Implementation guidance:
1. Reuse preserved lead intelligence rather than regenerating everything from scratch
2. Transform sales/deal intelligence into execution intelligence after conversion
3. Place this panel above the existing Client Execution Plan/workstream panels
4. Make it feel like the strategic brain for delivery, not just another status box

Important product rule:
The client workspace should not become a checklist-only environment.
It should retain a strategic intelligence layer that explains why the execution plan exists and what outcome it is trying to create.

Success criteria:
- client workspace has a clear execution-oriented intelligence layer
- sold scope is reflected as one coordinated strategy
- agents have clear strategic guidance for delivery
- website, SEO, and GBP workstreams feel connected rather than isolated
- the transition from lead intelligence to client execution feels continuous
