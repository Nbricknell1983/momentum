I want to improve the AI Sales Engine by adding a new educational insight block under:

Control the Call → AI Insights / Gaps

Purpose:
This section should help sales reps explain the difference between what a human sees on a website and what Google sees when evaluating whether a business deserves to rank.

The current website preview is visually useful, but it only shows what a person sees.
I want the system to also interpret the website from Google's perspective using sitemap data, crawl data, metadata, headings, page coverage, and topical/location relevance.

Core concept:
Humans see design, images, branding, and layout.
Google sees structure, content, pages, headings, internal links, services, locations, metadata, schema, and authority signals.

I want this section to educate the rep and also help them sell the need for a stronger online presence.

Build a new component called:
“How Google Sees Your Website”
or
“Human View vs Google View”

This component should sit inside AI Insights / Gaps and contain the following:

1. Human View
Show a short explanation like:
“A customer sees branding, images, and a nice-looking website. But Google does not interpret a website the same way a human does.”

2. Google View
Show a structured explanation like:
“Google evaluates whether it clearly understands:
- what you do
- where you do it
- how well each service is supported by dedicated pages
- how relevant your content is to search intent
- whether your website structure supports trust and discoverability”

3. Inputs the AI should use
Use the following data sources when available:
- sitemap pages captured
- crawl results
- homepage screenshot
- page titles
- meta descriptions
- H1s/H2s
- page URLs
- internal linking
- service pages
- location pages
- schema markup
- Google Business Profile data
- imported keyword data
- competitor keyword/service/location gaps

4. AI analysis output
The AI should generate a simple but persuasive analysis with sections like:

A. What humans see
Example:
“This site looks legitimate to a human visitor and has a clear brand presence.”

B. What Google sees
Example:
“Google currently sees only 8 indexed pages and limited service-location depth. That means Google may not fully understand all the services offered, where they are offered, or which searches the business should appear for.”

C. Key gaps
Examples:
- Too few pages to represent all services
- No dedicated location pages
- Weak heading structure
- Missing keyword alignment
- Limited topical depth
- No strong internal linking between services and locations
- Metadata not targeting discovery searches
- Insufficient schema or entity reinforcement

D. Why it matters
Examples:
- Google may not confidently rank the business for high-intent searches
- Competitors with clearer service/location signals may outrank them
- The website may look fine to people but still be weak as a search asset
- A good-looking site is not the same as a high-performing site

E. Recommended next step
Examples:
- Build dedicated service pages
- Build location pages
- strengthen H1/H2 and on-page copy
- improve metadata
- add schema
- create better internal linking
- align site structure to real keyword demand

5. Tone and sales intent
The tone should be consultative, intelligent, and persuasive.
It should help the rep control the call and explain:
“Your website may look fine visually, but Google needs more clarity before it can trust and rank you strongly.”

This should feel like insight, not a generic audit.
It should support NEPQ-style selling by helping create problem awareness without sounding pushy.

6. UI requirements
Make this component visually strong.
Use a two-column comparison layout:

Left side: “What Humans See”
- screenshot preview
- branding/design comments
- trust/appearance observations

Right side: “What Google Sees”
- sitemap count
- crawl depth
- page coverage
- missing services
- missing locations
- SEO structure observations
- content clarity score or visibility score

Below the comparison, show:
- AI Summary
- Key Gaps
- Why This Matters
- Recommended Actions

7. Dynamic logic
If only a screenshot exists, generate a limited visual-only insight.
If sitemap and crawl data exist, generate a stronger SEO/Google interpretation.
If keyword data is imported, compare keyword opportunities against existing page structure.
If competitor/service-area data exists, identify structural ranking gaps.

8. Example message style
Use messaging like:

“This website may look professional to a human visitor, but Google ranks based on clarity, coverage, and relevance. Right now, the site gives Google limited signals about the full range of services and target areas. That creates a visibility gap.”

or

“A human can recognise ABT Roofing because you already know the brand. Google cannot rely on brand familiarity the same way. It needs structured evidence about services, locations, and relevance.”

9. Goal
The goal is to help reps explain why a business needs:
- more structure
- more service/location depth
- better SEO foundations
- a website that works as a search asset, not just a brochure

Please build this as a reusable AI insight module inside the AI Sales Engine.
It must feel premium, intelligent, and sales-enabling.
